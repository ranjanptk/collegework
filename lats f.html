Latissimus Dorsi Exercises

Lat Pulldown (Cable)

Loading recommended sets/reps...

How to do it: Sit at the cable machine, grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width, pull down to your upper chest while squeezing your lats, then slowly release.

Benefits: Strengthens lats, improves posture, enhances pulling strength, and stabilizes your upper back.

Seated Row (Machine)

Loading recommended sets/reps...

How to do it: Sit at the rowing machine, grab the handle, pull it towards your torso while keeping your back straight, then slowly return.

Benefits: Engages the middle back, rhomboids, and lats, improves posture and back thickness.

Pull-ups

Loading recommended sets/reps...

How to do it: Grip the pull-up bar with palms facing away, pull your body up until your chin is over the bar, then slowly lower down.

Benefits: Strengthens lats, biceps, and grip; improves upper body pulling power and shoulder stability.

Dumbbell Rows

Loading recommended sets/reps...

How to do it: Place one knee on a bench, hold a dumbbell in the opposite hand, pull it towards your waist while keeping your back straight, then slowly lower.

Benefits: Targets each side individually, increases lat thickness, stabilizes spine, improves back symmetry.

Barbell Rows

Loading recommended sets/reps...

How to do it: Hold a barbell with palms facing down, hinge at the hips, pull the barbell towards your lower chest, then slowly lower it.

Benefits: Builds overall back strength, works lats, traps, and rear delts, improves posture and upper body power.
